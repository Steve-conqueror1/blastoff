If you own or manage a commercial kitchen in Alberta, **NFPA 96** is a term you must understand. Developed by the National Fire Protection Association, NFPA 96 is the standard for ventilation control and fire protection of commercial cooking operations.

This standard outlines strict rules for the design, installation, operation, inspection, and maintenance of kitchen exhaust systems to minimize the risk of fire.

## Key Areas Covered by NFPA 96

NFPA 96 is comprehensive, regulating everything from the hood filters to the fan on the roof. Here are the most critical requirements for business owners:

### 1. Complete System Cleaning
NFPA 96 Section 11.6.1 states that the entire exhaust system must be cleaned by a certified professional. A complete cleaning is not just wiping down the hood. It includes:
* **The Hood:** Wash and degrease interior and exterior surfaces.
* **The Grease Filters:** Clean or replace baffle filters.
* **The Horizontal and Vertical Ducts:** Clean the entire path where grease travels.
* **The Exhaust Fan:** Clean the fan blades, housing, and the grease drip tray on the roof.

### 2. Access Panels
You cannot clean what you cannot reach. NFPA 96 requires that grease ducts have access panels installed at regular intervals (especially at changes of direction) so that technicians can inspect and clean the entire interior. If your ductwork lacks access panels, they must be professionally installed.

### 3. Roof Fan Hinge Kit
Exhaust fans on the roof are heavy and must be tilted back to clean the blades and the vertical duct below. NFPA 96 requires that these fans be equipped with a heavy-duty hinge kit. Without a hinge, tilting the fan can damage electrical wiring, warp the fan shroud, or injure the technician.

### 4. Certified Cleaners & Documentation
Cleanings must be performed by trained, qualified, and certified persons. Once completed, a certificate or sticker showing the cleaning date, next scheduled date, and the name of the servicing company must be posted on the hood. A copy of the service report must also be kept on-site for inspection by the local fire marshal or insurance adjusters.

## What Happens if You Are Not Compliant?

Failing to meet NFPA 96 standards can lead to severe consequences:
1. **Fire Marshal Violations:** You can face fines, citations, or immediate shutdown orders during routine fire inspections.
2. **Extreme Fire Hazard:** 90% of kitchen fires spread through the exhaust system. Excess grease in the ducts turns your system into a fuse that spreads fire to the roof.
3. **Insurance Claim Denial:** Insurance providers frequently request proof of regular professional exhaust cleaning. Without NFPA 96 documentation, they may deny coverage after a fire.
